# Boosted Trumpet Initial Data


This poster was presented at Amaldi/GR 2026 and was awarded a Best Poster Prize by the conference organisers.

#### Abstract

Improving the accuracy and efficiency of numerical relativity simulations is a serious
challenge for gravitational wave astronomy. One potential improvement would be to
construct initial data in a gauge better suited to numerical evolution. For moving-
puncture simulations we know the analytic solution for the Schwarzschild spacetime
in 1+log slicing, but the corresponding solution for boosted and/or spinning black
holes has been a long-standing problem in the field. In this work we present first steps
to solve the axisymmetric Einstein equations for advecting Bowen-York trumpet data,
first in Maximal slicing and then in 1+log slicing.

I used a soft-pastel on blackboard aesthetic. This is a deliberate nod to the analytical nature of the project, while also framing the lengthy equations in the style of a lecture board in an attempt to make the intimidating mathematics more approachable. It’s also a slightly tongue-in-cheek play on the back-to-basics look of a primary school classroom, contrasted against the distinctly non-primary-school complexity of constructing black hole initial data. The central focal point of the design is a flow chart demonstrating the cyclic nature of the initial data construction process, and I coded the key variables to try and aid the viewer in tracking them through the different steps of the solve. Coincidentally, this variable colouring approach is also used in "Numerical Relativity: Starting from Scratch" by Baumgarte and Shapiro, which I purchased with the book voucher that I was awarded with this prize!
